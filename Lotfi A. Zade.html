Lotfi A. Zadeh

Lutfi Aliasker Zadeh was a mathematician, computer scientist, electrical engineer, artificial intelligence researcher and professor of computer science at the University of California, Berkeley. Zadeh is famous for proposing fuzzy mathematics.

About the Super-brain

☛ Zadeh was born on February 4,1921 in Baku,Azerbaijan as Lotfi Aliaskerzadeh.

☛ His father was Rahim Aleskerzade, an Iranian Muslim Azerbaijani journalist from Ardabil on assignment from Iran and his mother was Fanya Korenman, a Jewish pediatrician from Odessa, Ukraine, who was an Iranian citizen.

☛ Today, 6 important theories of Lutfi Zadeh are known to world science. Currently, they are widely used in science and industry. His theory of fuzzy logic, which is considered a revolution in world science, brought him world fame.

☛ This theory is a new expression for the concept of binary set, which is the basis of mathematics: fuzzy set. It allows for the inclusion of fuzzy dimensions in science and the uncertainty of risk in society to be more adequately captured.

☛This theory, applied by leading world companies, was developed in 1965. The theory has long accepted American scientific development, and it was only in the 80s of the century that it attracted the attention of Japanese scientists, and the Japanese came to take advantage of this unique theory. The application of Lutfi Zade's theory has earned the country billions. Today, Japan's Mitsubishi, Toshiba, Sony, Canon, Sanyo, Nissan, Honda and other reputable companies are manufacturing photo and video cameras, washing machines, and vacuum cleaners based on fuzzy logic theory , widely used in the management of cars, trains, industrial processes. In 1989, Lutfi Zadeh was awarded with the highest award given to Japanese scientists - "Honda" award for the success of the theory of fuzzy logic in the industry. Americans have also begun to understand the value of this theory and take advantage of it. Today, this theory is widely applied in the production of the American companies "General Motors", "General Electric", "Motorola", "Dupont", "Kodak" and others. Currently, this theory is used in economics, psychology, linguistics, politics, philosophy, sociology, religious administration, and conflict problems.

☛In addition to the theory of fuzzy logic, Lutfi Zade offers 5 fundamental scientific theories: "theory of impressions", "theory of systems", "theory of word processing computer", "theory of optimal filters" and "soft computing".
